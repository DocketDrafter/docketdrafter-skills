# Default Judgments and Judgments by Confession

## Issue

MCA defendants often first appear after a default judgment, restraint, or
judgment by confession. The procedural route matters: a default judgment entered
after nonappearance is usually attacked by motion under CPLR 317 or CPLR 5015,
while a judgment by confession often requires a separate plenary action unless a
recognized exception applies.

Read CPLR 317, CPLR 3218, and CPLR 5015 before giving procedural advice.

## CPLR 317

CPLR 317 applies when the summons was not personally delivered to the defendant
or its designated agent for service. The movant must show lack of personal
receipt of notice in time to defend and a meritorious defense. The motion must
be made within one year after the defendant learns of the judgment and no more
than five years after entry.

Use this route when service was made by Secretary of State, substituted service,
or another non-personal method and the defendant can show it did not actually
learn of the suit in time.

## CPLR 5015(a)(1): Excusable Default

The usual rule requires both a reasonable excuse and a meritorious defense.
Courts may deny vacatur without reaching the usury defense if the excuse is
weak. Settlement discussions, delayed retention of counsel, or unsupported law
office failure may not be enough.

## CPLR 5015(a)(4): Lack of Jurisdiction

A jurisdictional default-vacatur motion is different from excusable default.
Where the court lacked personal jurisdiction, vacatur is not discretionary in
the same way, and the one-year limit applicable to CPLR 5015(a)(1) does not
control. In MCA cases, analyze individual guarantors separately from the merchant
entity.

## Interest-of-Justice Vacatur

CPLR 5015(a) is not exhaustive. A court may vacate a default for sufficient
reason and in the interests of substantial justice. *Crystal Springs* is the key
MCA example: the defendants established that the agreement was a criminally
usurious loan, and the Second Department vacated the judgment, dismissed the
complaint, and vacated the restraining notice.

## Judgments by Confession

A party seeking to vacate a judgment entered on an affidavit of confession of
judgment generally must commence a separate plenary action. In that action,
allegations that the MCA was a usurious loan, that the confession papers were
false, or that the judgment was procured by fraud or misconduct may state a
cognizable claim. Do not assume CPLR 5015(a)(3) itself creates an independent
cause of action; it is a procedural vacatur mechanism.

## Defendant-Favorable Authorities

- [Crystal Springs Capital, Inc. v. Big Thicket Coin, LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/9432148/crystal-springs-capital-inc-v-big-thicket-coin-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-9432148-crystal-springs-capital-inc-v-big-thicket-coin-llc/>)), 220 A.D.3d 745 (2d Dept. 2023) — Default judgment vacated in the interest of justice because the agreement was a criminally usurious loan. Also granted CPLR 3211(a) dismissal and vacated a CPLR 5222 restraint under CPLR 5240.
- [SOS Capital v. Recycling Paper Partners of PA, LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/9423832/sos-capital-v-recycling-paper-partners-of-pa-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-9423832-sos-capital-v-recycling-paper-partners-of-pa-llc/>)), 220 A.D.3d 25 (1st Dept. 2023) — Individual defendants obtained CPLR 5015(a)(4) vacatur and dismissal for lack of personal jurisdiction. Corporate defendant did not obtain discretionary vacatur because it lacked a reasonable excuse.
- [Oakshire Props., LLC v. Argus Capital Funding, LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/10015571/oakshire-props-llc-v-argus-capital-funding-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-10015571-oakshire-props-llc-v-argus-capital-funding-llc/>)), 2024 NY Slip Op 03943 (4th Dept. July 26, 2024) — Plenary action to vacate a judgment by confession survived dismissal. The court recognized a cognizable claim where plaintiffs alleged fraud, misrepresentation, misconduct, and falsity in the confession papers.
- [K9 Bytes, Inc. v. Arch Capital Funding, LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/6318781/k9-bytes-inc-v-arch-capital-funding-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-6318781-k9-bytes-inc-v-arch-capital-funding-llc/>)), 56 Misc. 3d 807 (Sup. Ct. Westchester Cnty. 2017) — Plenary action may seek to vacate confessions of judgment based on usury and related wrongful conduct.
- [Funding Metrics, LLC v. NRO BOSTON, LLC](<https://scholar.google.com/scholar_case?case=12517471399310218774>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/gs-2019-ny-slip-op-32651/>)), 2019 NY Slip Op 32651(U) (Sup. Ct. Westchester Cnty. Aug. 28, 2019) — Trial court vacated judgments by confession under CPLR 5015(a)(3) where the MCA agreements were criminally usurious on their face.

## Limiting / Funder-Favorable Authorities

- [Clearfund Solutions LLC v. Tomassetti](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/9474006/clearfund-solutions-llc-v-tomassetti/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-9474006-clearfund-solutions-llc-v-tomassetti/>)), 2024 NY Slip Op 00729 (4th Dept. Feb. 9, 2024) — Vacatur denied where defendants failed to establish a reasonable excuse; the court did not need to reach whether they had a meritorious usury defense.
- [Diesel Funding, LLC v. Build Retail, Inc.](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/10851010/diesel-funding-llc-v-build-retail-inc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-10851010-diesel-funding-llc-v-build-retail-inc/>)), 2026 NY Slip Op 02629 (2d Dept. Apr. 29, 2026) — Vacatur and settlement challenge rejected because the MCA was a valid purchase, not a criminally usurious loan.
- [Champion Auto Sales, LLC v. Pearl Beta Funding, LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/4477821/champion-auto-sales-llc-v-pearl-beta-funding-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-4477821-champion-auto-sales-llc-v-pearl-beta-funding-llc/>)), 159 A.D.3d 507 (1st Dept. 2018) — Complaint seeking to vacate a judgment by confession dismissed where the underlying agreement was not usurious.
