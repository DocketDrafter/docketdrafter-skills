# Defense-Side Motions to Dismiss

## Issue

A merchant defendant may try to dispose of an MCA collection complaint at the
pleading stage, usually by arguing that the agreement is a criminally usurious
loan, that documentary evidence defeats the complaint, or that the court lacks
jurisdiction over an out-of-state merchant or guarantor. Read CPLR 3211 and the
loan-vs-purchase topic before drafting or analyzing the motion.

## Main Dismissal Lanes

### CPLR 3211(a)(1): Documentary Evidence

Use this where the agreement itself establishes that repayment is absolute. The
best defense-side model is *Crystal Springs*: the agreement required a fixed
daily debit, made reconciliation optional for the funder, and allowed collection
of the full uncollected balance plus fees on default or bankruptcy. The Second
Department held those terms established a criminally usurious loan and directed
dismissal.

This is strongest when the agreement is complete, authenticated, and facially
one-sided. If the agreement has mixed signals, dismissal may fail because loan
status cannot be determined as a matter of law.

### CPLR 3211(a)(7): Failure to State a Claim

Use this where the complaint itself omits necessary facts about the contract,
performance, breach, damages, guaranty liability, or standing. This topic is
less developed in MCA-specific appellate law than the usury/documentary-evidence
lane, so also read the breach-proof topic for ordinary contract elements.

### CPLR 3211(a)(2), (a)(8), and BCL § 1314

Out-of-state MCA cases often raise subject-matter, capacity, and personal
jurisdiction issues. These arguments are fact-sensitive and may turn on where
the agreement was made, where funding was sent from, whether the funder is a
foreign entity, forum-selection language, and whether the individual guarantor
personally transacted business in New York.

## Defendant-Favorable Authorities

- [Crystal Springs Capital, Inc. v. Big Thicket Coin, LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/9432148/crystal-springs-capital-inc-v-big-thicket-coin-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-9432148-crystal-springs-capital-inc-v-big-thicket-coin-llc/>)), 220 A.D.3d 745 (2d Dept. 2023) — Best defense-side CPLR 3211(a)(1) model. The agreement itself established a criminally usurious loan: fixed daily debit, no meaningful reconciliation obligation, and full recourse on default or bankruptcy. The court vacated the default judgment, dismissed the complaint, and vacated a restraining notice.
- [Oakshire Props., LLC v. Argus Capital Funding, LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/10015571/oakshire-props-llc-v-argus-capital-funding-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-10015571-oakshire-props-llc-v-argus-capital-funding-llc/>)), 2024 NY Slip Op 03943 (4th Dept. July 26, 2024) — Funder's CPLR 3211 motion denied. The complaint sufficiently alleged a usurious loan where reconciliation appeared illusory, the daily payment implied a finite term, and the default/JOC structure supplied recourse. Useful for opposing a funder's motion to dismiss and for drafting allegations in a plenary action.
- [Davis v. Richmond Capital Group, LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/4884094/davis-v-richmond-capital-group-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-4884094-davis-v-richmond-capital-group-llc/>)), 194 A.D.3d 516 (1st Dept. 2021) — Merchants sufficiently alleged disguised-loan claims based on discretionary reconciliation, refused reconciliation, unrealistic daily payments, rapid default triggers, and guaranty/bankruptcy recourse.
- [SOS Capital v. Recycling Paper Partners of PA, LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/9423832/sos-capital-v-recycling-paper-partners-of-pa-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-9423832-sos-capital-v-recycling-paper-partners-of-pa-llc/>)), 220 A.D.3d 25 (1st Dept. 2023) — Personal-jurisdiction defense succeeded for individual defendants after default. Remote communications and out-of-state conduct by corporate principals did not establish CPLR 302(a)(2) jurisdiction over them individually.

## Limiting / Funder-Favorable Authorities

- [Kapitus Servicing, Inc. v. Point Blank Constr., Inc.](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/9447246/kapitus-servicing-inc-v-point-blank-constr-inc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-9447246-kapitus-servicing-inc-v-point-blank-constr-inc/>)), 2023 NY Slip Op 06087 (1st Dept. Nov. 28, 2023) — Defendants failed to obtain dismissal where loan status could not be gleaned from the face of the agreement as a matter of law. The agreement had both loan-like and purchase-like features.
- [Pearl Beta Funding, LLC v. Elegant Trio Colors Corp.](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/10578972/pearl-beta-funding-llc-v-elegant-trio-colors-corp/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-10578972-pearl-beta-funding-llc-v-elegant-trio-colors-corp/>)), 2025 NY Slip Op 02217 (2d Dept. Apr. 16, 2025) — Trial court dismissed under CPLR 3211(a)(2)/BCL § 1314, but the Second Department reversed. The funder's submissions were enough at the pleading stage to show New York could exercise jurisdiction.
- [Fundfi Merchant Funding, LLC v. BKT High Quality Healthcare Agency LLC](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/9459454/fundfi-merchant-funding-llc-v-bkt-high-quality-healthcare-agency-llc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-9459454-fundfi-merchant-funding-llc-v-bkt-high-quality-healthcare-agency-llc/>)), 2024 NY Slip Op 24006 (Sup. Ct. Kings Cnty. Jan. 10, 2024) — Defendant motion to dismiss based on jurisdiction and alleged forged MCA signature denied without prejudice pending discovery. Useful caution where authority, ratification, funding, or benefit from proceeds are fact-bound.
- [Diesel Funding, LLC v. Build Retail, Inc.](<https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/10851010/diesel-funding-llc-v-build-retail-inc/>) ([bundle](<../references/cases/cl-10851010-diesel-funding-llc-v-build-retail-inc/>)), 2026 NY Slip Op 02629 (2d Dept. Apr. 29, 2026) — Funder-side result on vacatur and settlement enforcement; defendants' usury argument failed because reconciliation and contingent-repayment features showed a valid purchase.
