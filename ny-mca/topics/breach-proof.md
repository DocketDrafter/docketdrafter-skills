# Breach Proof and Summary Judgment Mechanics

## Issue

Even where an MCA plaintiff defeats a usury defense or establishes that the
agreement is a valid purchase of receivables, summary judgment is not automatic.
The plaintiff still bears the ordinary burden of proving each element of breach
of contract with admissible evidence: the contract, plaintiff's performance,
defendant's breach, and resulting damages. If plaintiff's own submissions are
internally inconsistent, conclusory, or leave room for a factual dispute on
damages or nonperformance, the motion should fail regardless of the strength of
the usury response (*Bridge Funding*; *Winegrad*).

This topic concerns the separate question whether a plaintiff has actually
proved breach and damages on the motion record, apart from whether the
agreement is enforceable.

## Governing Rule

The motion still turns on the ordinary elements of breach of contract:
contract, plaintiff's performance, defendant's breach, and resulting damages.
If the movant fails to make a prima facie showing, the motion must be denied
regardless of the sufficiency of the opposing papers (*Winegrad*).

## Core Rule

- [Winegrad v. New York University Medical Center](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Winegrad v. New York University Medical Center.md>) — Court of Appeals; if the movant fails to make a prima facie showing, the motion must be denied regardless of the sufficiency of the opposing papers

## Defendant-Side Authorities

- [Bridge Funding Cap LLC v. SimonExpress Pizza, LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Bridge Funding Cap LLC v. SimonExpress Pizza, LLC.md>), 2025 NY Slip Op 04306 (4th Dept. July 25, 2025) — Plaintiff established that the agreement was a valid revenue purchase rather than a loan, but still lost summary judgment because its own submissions created a triable issue on damages; the manager's affidavit conflicted with the verified complaint on the amount due and no explanation was offered
- [Tapp Partners, LLC v. Wall Sections Inc.](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Tapp Partners, LLC v. Wall Sections Inc..md>), 2025 NY Slip Op 07126 (4th Dept. Dec. 23, 2025) — Plaintiff met its initial burden with the agreement, bank statements, and manager affidavit, but defendants defeated summary judgment by raising a triable issue regarding damages through Garrett's affidavit; guaranty liability rose or fell with the contract claim

## Plaintiff-Side Comparators

- [Apollo Funding Co. v. Dave Reilly Constr., LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Apollo Funding Co. v. Dave Reilly Constr., LLC.md>), 2025 NY Slip Op 05055 (2d Dept. Sept. 24, 2025) — Plaintiff submitted proof of the executed agreement, its own performance, defendants' breach, and damages; once the usury defense failed, summary judgment was granted
- [True Bus. Funding, LLC v. Guerrero A Constr. Corp.](<../references/cases/courtlistener/True Bus. Funding, LLC v. Guerrero A Constr. Corp..md>), 2025 NY Slip Op 03598 (2d Dept. June 11, 2025) — Plaintiff prevailed on summary judgment after establishing the transaction was a purchase of receivables rather than a loan and defendant failed to raise a triable issue

## Emerging Pattern

- The 2025 Fourth Department cases give visible attention to ordinary breach-proof requirements even when the MCA agreement survives usury scrutiny
- Damages disputes alone may defeat summary judgment
- Guaranty liability follows the underlying contract claim
- The recent Second Department comparators are more plaintiff-favorable where the motion papers cleanly establish performance, breach, and damages and the opposition does not raise a factual dispute
