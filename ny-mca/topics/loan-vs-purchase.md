# Loan vs. Purchase — The Three-Factor Test

## Issue

An MCA funder labels its agreement a "purchase of future receivables," but the
defendant argues the transaction is really a loan — and if the interest rate
exceeds 25%, it is criminally usurious and void ab initio under GOL § 5-511 and
Penal Law § 190.40. Corporate borrowers can raise criminal usury (*Adar Bays*).

## Governing Framework

Courts look past labels and examine the transaction "in its totality" to
determine whether the funder is "absolutely entitled to repayment under all
circumstances" (*LG Funding*). Three guideposts:

1. **Reconciliation** — Does the agreement allow the merchant to adjust payments
   based on actual revenue, or is the reconciliation provision illusory?
2. **Finite term** — Are payments structured to repay a fixed sum over a
   calculable period, or is the term genuinely indefinite?
3. **Bankruptcy** — Does the merchant's bankruptcy extinguish the obligation, or
   does the funder retain full recourse?

These factors are a guide, not a checklist. A transaction can be a loan even if
only two are present (*Advance Servs. v. Acadian Props.*; *Fleetwood*). The
"essential question" remains whether repayment is absolute (*Fleetwood*).

## Current Shape of the Case Law

The 2d Department has been largely funder-friendly from *Principis* (2022)
through 2025–2026, finding valid purchases where the agreement had a
reconciliation provision, indefinite term, and no bankruptcy-as-default clause —
especially where the defendant **never invoked reconciliation** (*Apollo
Funding*; *Diesel Funding*). But the same court will still recharacterize an
agreement as a criminally usurious loan where the reconciliation right is absent
or optional and the balance is absolutely due (*Crystal Springs*).

The 1st Department has left more room for the usury defense — *Davis v. Richmond
Capital* (2021) sustained loan allegations built on discretionary reconciliation
and guaranty recourse, and *Kapitus v. Ragtime Gourmet* (2025) found triable
issues where the agreement provided recourse upon bankruptcy and a personal
guaranty with a security interest.

The strongest defendant-side authorities remain *Fleetwood* (detailed illusory-
reconciliation analysis, 99.8–278.5% interest) and *Richmond Capital* (AG
enforcement action, both trial and appellate). *Funding Metrics* and *Advance
Servs.* are useful trial-level complements because they show courts finding
loan-like repayment obligations even where the MCA papers used purchase
terminology.

## Foundational Cases

- [Seidel v. 18 East 17th Street Owners, Inc.](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Seidel v. 18 East 17th Street Owners, Inc..md>), 79 N.Y.2d 735 (1992) — Court of Appeals; source of the "if the transaction is not a loan, there can be no usury, however unconscionable the contract may be" rule that funders quote. But read it in full: the court looks to substance, not form — "if the court can see that the real transaction was the loan or forbearance of money at usurious interest, its plain and imperative duty is to so declare." Also lays out the consequences of usury — the borrower keeps the principal and the securing instruments are cancelled
- [Blue Wolf Capital Fund II, L.P. v. American Stevedoring, Inc.](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Blue Wolf Capital Fund II, L.P. v. American Stevedoring, Inc..md>), 105 A.D.3d 178 (1st Dept. 2013) — Not an MCA case (a secured demand loan), but the leading substance-over-form authority: usury is judged "by its substance or real character," and a contingent payment left to the *lender's* sole discretion counts as interest. A criminally usurious loan is void and the lender gets no reformation (unclean hands). Confirms a corporate borrower may raise criminal usury where the loan is $250K–$2.5M (GOL § 5-521(3))
- [LG Funding, LLC v. United Senior Props. of Olathe, LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/LG Funding, LLC v. United Senior Props. of Olathe, LLC.md>), 181 A.D.3d 664 (2d Dept. 2020) — Leading appellate statement of the three-factor test for loan-vs-purchase analysis
- [Adar Bays LLC v. GeneSYS ID, Inc.](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Adar Bays v. GeneSYS ID.md>), 37 N.Y.3d 320 (2021) — Court of Appeals; criminal usury defense available to corporate borrowers; usurious loans void ab initio
- [K9 Bytes, Inc. v. Arch Capital Funding, LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/K9 Bytes, Inc. v. Arch Capital Funding, LLC.md>), 56 Misc. 3d 807 (Sup. Ct. Westchester Cnty. 2017) — Trial-court origin of the three-factor test later adopted by *LG Funding* and *Principis*. Side by side, it found the funder's agreements *with* a reconciliation provision were not loans, but a near-identical agreement *without* one could not be dismissed as a matter of law. Also holds a corporation may raise criminal usury only as a defense, not as an affirmative claim, and that unconscionability is a defense, not a cause of action

## Transaction Found to Be a Loan (Defendant Wins)

- [Crystal Springs Capital, Inc. v. Big Thicket Coin, LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Crystal Springs Capital, Inc. v. Big Thicket Coin, LLC.md>), 220 A.D.3d 745 (2d Dept. 2023) — Criminally usurious loan: a fixed $4,000 daily debit, the funder "under no obligation" to reconcile to a percentage of sales, and the full uncollected balance due on default or bankruptcy — repayment was absolute. Procedurally important: the merchant won on a CPLR 3211(a)(1) documentary-evidence motion (the agreement itself "utterly refutes" the funder's allegations) and vacated a default judgment under CPLR 5015(a) "in the interest of justice"
- [Fleetwood Servs., LLC v. Ram Capital Funding, LLC](<../references/cases/justia/Fleetwood Services, LLC v. Ram Capital Funding, LLC.md>), 2022 U.S. Dist. LEXIS 100837 (S.D.N.Y. June 6, 2022) — Detailed application of three-factor test; illusory reconciliation; "essential question" is whether funder is absolutely entitled to repayment; usurious interest rate of 99.8–278.5%
- [People v. Richmond Capital Group LLC (Trial Ct.)](<../references/cases/justia/People v. Richmond Capital Group LLC (Trial Ct.).md>), 2023 NY Slip Op 33194(U) (Sup. Ct. N.Y. Cnty. Sept. 15, 2023) — AG enforcement action; detailed loan-vs-purchase analysis after multi-day hearing; "risk of ownership" and "obligation to repay is absolute" framing
- [People v. Richmond Capital Group LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/People v. Richmond Capital Group LLC.md>), 2026 NY Slip Op 00990 (1st Dept. 2026) — First Department affirmance of trial court's loan-vs-purchase recharacterization
- [Funding Metrics, LLC v. NRO BOSTON, LLC](<../references/cases/google-scholar/Funding Metrics, LLC v. NRO BOSTON, LLC.md>), 2019 NY Slip Op 32651(U) (Sup. Ct. Westchester Cnty. Aug. 28, 2019) — Finds the purported receivables purchase criminally usurious where the purchased amount was absolutely repayable, the daily remittances exceeded or nearly exhausted receivables, and the guaranty structure belied any real transfer of risk
- [Advance Servs. Group LLC v. Acadian Props. Austin LLC](<../references/cases/justia/Advance Servs. Group LLC v. Acadian Props. Austin LLC.md>), 2021 NY Slip Op 50221(U) (Sup. Ct. Kings Cnty. Mar. 12, 2021) — Summary judgment for funder denied and defendants granted dismissal; useful for the point that a court may find a disguised loan even without every factor aligning perfectly

## Transaction Found to Be a Valid Purchase (Funder Wins)

- [Champion Auto Sales, LLC v. Pearl Beta Funding, LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Champion Auto Sales, LLC v. Pearl Beta Funding, LLC.md>), 159 A.D.3d 507 (1st Dept. 2018) — The first appellate decision holding an MCA receivables-purchase agreement is not a usurious transaction. Brief; affirmed dismissal of a complaint that sought to vacate a judgment by confession. Funders cite it as the start of the appellate line — but note how thin the analysis is
- [Principis Capital, LLC v. I Do, Inc.](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Principis Capital, LLC v. I Do, Inc..md>), 201 A.D.3d 752 (2d Dept. 2022) — Applied the three-factor test and found a valid purchase: payments adjusted with monthly sales, term not finite, no bankruptcy-as-default provision. Procedurally notable — the funder won summary judgment *on the complaint and dismissing the affirmative defenses and counterclaims*. The most heavily cited funder-side authority in recent Fora-type briefs
- [True Bus. Funding, LLC v. Guerrero A Constr. Corp.](<../references/cases/courtlistener/True Bus. Funding, LLC v. Guerrero A Constr. Corp..md>), 2025 NY Slip Op 03598 (2d Dept. June 11, 2025) — Not a loan: reconciliation provision allowed payment adjustments, term was indefinite, and bankruptcy was a valid excuse that did not trigger default or guaranty
- [Apollo Funding Co. v. Dave Reilly Constr., LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Apollo Funding Co. v. Dave Reilly Constr., LLC.md>), 2025 NY Slip Op 05055 (2d Dept. Sept. 24, 2025) — Not a loan: payments adjusted based on daily receipts, indefinite term, bankruptcy not a default; defendant's failure to invoke reconciliation procedure precluded "illusory reconciliation" argument
- [Spin Capital, LLC v. Bridgelink Engg., LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Spin Capital, LLC v. Bridgelink Engg., LLC.md>), 2026 NY Slip Op 02296 (2d Dept. Apr. 15, 2026) — Not a loan: reconciliation provision, indefinite term, bankruptcy not a default; also dismissed defendants' RICO counterclaims
- [Diesel Funding, LLC v. Build Retail, Inc.](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Diesel Funding, LLC v. Build Retail, Inc..md>), 2026 NY Slip Op 02629 (2d Dept. Apr. 29, 2026) — Not a loan under three-factor test; defendants never invoked reconciliation, precluding "illusory" argument (citing Apollo Funding)

## Triable Issues of Fact

- [Davis v. Richmond Capital Group, LLC](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Davis v. Richmond Capital Group, LLC.md>), 194 A.D.3d 516 (1st Dept. 2021) — Motion to dismiss denied; merchants sufficiently alleged the MCA agreements were disguised loans based on the *discretionary* nature of the reconciliation provisions, the funder's refusal to permit reconciliation, daily payment rates that were not a good-faith estimate of receivables, two or three rejected debits triggering an event of default and immediate full repayment, and a guaranty collectible on the merchant's inability to pay or bankruptcy. The key 1st Dept statement on illusory/discretionary reconciliation and guaranty structure
- [Kapitus Servicing, Inc. v. Ragtime Gourmet Corp.](<../references/cases/courtlistener/Kapitus Servicing, Inc. v. Ragtime Gourmet Corp..md>), 2025 NY Slip Op 05921 (1st Dept. Oct. 28, 2025) — Summary judgment denied; triable issues on usury where agreement provided recourse upon bankruptcy and personal guaranty with security interest, despite indefinite term and percentage-based collection

## Recurring Tensions

- Whether a reconciliation provision is only facially present or is practically meaningful
- Whether an apparently indefinite term is genuinely contingent or merely a fixed remittance schedule
- Whether bankruptcy/default provisions, guaranties, or security interests eliminate the funder's supposed risk
- How much weight to give nonuse of reconciliation procedures in recent 2d Department cases

## Calculating the Rate

If the transaction is a loan, compute the effective annual interest rate to
determine whether it exceeds the 25% criminal usury threshold. Read
[Usury Calculation](<usury-calculation.md>) before performing any APR math.
