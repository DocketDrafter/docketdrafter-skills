In an appeal from Supreme Court, New York County, the court unanimously affirmed the
denial of plaintiff Kapitus Servicing, Inc.’s motion for summary judgment in an action
arising from a merchant cash advance agreement with defendant Ragtime Gourmet Corp. The
court found disputed issues of fact as to whether the agreement constituted a criminally
usurious loan or an enforceable sale and purchase of receivables. Key indicators
included bankruptcy recourse provisions and a personal guaranty suggesting a loan,
contrasted with indefinite payment terms and collection as a percentage of receipts
supporting a sale. Jo-Le Holding was properly dismissed for lack of service.
