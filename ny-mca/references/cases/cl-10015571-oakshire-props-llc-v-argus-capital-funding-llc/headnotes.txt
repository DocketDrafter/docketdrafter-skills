In an appeal from an order denying in part defendants’ motion to dismiss the amended
complaint, the Fourth Department affirmed. Plaintiffs sought to vacate an ex parte
judgment by confession related to a transaction styled as the sale of future receipts
but alleged to be a usurious loan. Applying relevant pleading standards and usury law,
the court held the complaint sufficiently pleaded fraud and that the transaction’s
substance indicated an absolute obligation to repay, meeting criteria for a loan subject
to usury laws. The court declined to consider certain new arguments raised in
defendants’ reply papers but exercised discretion to address a purely legal issue
regarding the procedural means to vacate the confession judgment.
