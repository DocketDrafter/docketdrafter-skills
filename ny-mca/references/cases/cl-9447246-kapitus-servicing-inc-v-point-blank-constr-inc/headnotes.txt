In this appeal from an order denying defendants' motion to dismiss a complaint, the
Appellate Division, First Department, addressed subject matter jurisdiction and the
nature of a revenue-based factoring agreement dispute. The court held that jurisdiction
was proper under Business Corporation Law § 1314(b)(1) because the contract was made in
New York, the place of making being where the last act necessary for formation
occurred—here, plaintiff’s sending funds from New York to defendant’s Florida bank
account. The court also found defendants did not establish dismissal was warranted based
on a claim of criminal usury, as the agreement's character as either a loan or a sale of
receivables was not clear on its face. The order was unanimously affirmed.
