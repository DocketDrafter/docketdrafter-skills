In a consolidated appeal, defendants moved to dismiss various claims in an amended
complaint alleging breach of Merchant Cash Advance agreements, usury, RICO violations,
and deceptive trade practices. The court modified and granted dismissal of constructive
trust, accounting, and certain General Business Law claims, and dismissed contract
claims against individuals not parties to the agreements due to insufficient
veil-piercing allegations. It upheld breach of contract claims against the corporate
defendants, found sufficient usury-related allegations affecting enforceability, and
sustained the RICO claim only insofar as based on unlawful debt collection. The court
also struck prejudicial allegations unrelated to the claims and otherwise affirmed the
order without costs.
