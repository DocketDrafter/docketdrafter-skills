In an appeal from an order denying a motion to vacate a May 25, 2023 judgment and a
stipulation of settlement, the Appellate Division, Second Department, addressed whether
a contract purchasing future receivables constituted a usurious loan. The plaintiff
purchased an interest in the defendant's future receivables with payment capped and
adjusted based on sales, and a guaranty was executed. The court applied the totality
test to determine if the agreement was a loan subject to usury laws and held the
defendants failed to establish usury, noting the payment cap, adjustable payments,
nonfinite term, and lack of an event of default upon bankruptcy. The order was affirmed.
