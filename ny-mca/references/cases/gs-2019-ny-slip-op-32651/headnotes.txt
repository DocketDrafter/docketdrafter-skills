In consolidated motions pursuant to CPLR § 5015(3), defendants sought to vacate
Judgments by Confession entered against them on grounds of fraud, misrepresentation,
misconduct, and violation of criminal usury laws. The dispute involved Merchant
Agreements under which plaintiff advanced funds purportedly for the purchase of
defendants’ future receivables, with fixed daily ACH payments resulting in annualized
interest rates exceeding 90%. The court found the Agreements evidenced criminal usurious
loans due to absolute repayment obligations with calculated interest exceeding the legal
rate, lack of risk to plaintiff, and the presence of guaranties. The court granted the
motions and vacated the judgments as void ab initio.
