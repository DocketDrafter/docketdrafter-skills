In a special proceeding under Executive Law § 63(12), the Supreme Court, New York
County, granted the Attorney General’s amended petition against Richmond Capital Group
LLC, Ram Capital Funding LLC, Viceroy Capital Funding Inc., and individual respondents
(collectively, the Predatory Lenders). The Court held that the merchant cash advance
agreements were loans, not sales, and were criminally usurious, charging interest rates
exceeding the legal limit. It found the Predatory Lenders engaged in pervasive fraud,
submitted false affidavits, and that the loan documents were procedurally and
substantively unconscionable. Mr. Braun, RCG, and Ram were found to have harassed
borrowers. The Predatory Lenders were permanently enjoined and ordered to rescind
agreements, cease collections, pay restitution, vacate judgments, and pay $2,000 each in
costs. Claims against other respondents for harassment were dismissed.
