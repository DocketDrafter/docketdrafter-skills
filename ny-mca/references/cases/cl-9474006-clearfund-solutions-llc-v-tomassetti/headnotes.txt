In a breach of contract and unjust enrichment action under a merchant cash advance
agreement, defendants appealed from an order denying their motion to vacate a default
judgment entered after their failure to appear or answer. Applying CPLR 5015(a)(1), the
appellate court held that the Supreme Court did not abuse its discretion in denying
relief because defendants failed to establish a reasonable excuse for the default,
having abandoned their improper service claim on appeal. Because no reasonable excuse
was shown, the court declined to address whether defendants had a meritorious defense
and affirmed the order denying the motion without costs.
