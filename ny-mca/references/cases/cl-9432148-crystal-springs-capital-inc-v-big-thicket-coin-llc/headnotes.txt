In an appeal from an order denying the defendants’ motion to vacate a default judgment,
dismiss the complaint, and vacate a restraining notice, the Appellate Division held that
the Supreme Court should have granted the motion branches to vacate the judgment in the
interest of justice and to dismiss the complaint based on the contractual agreement
constituting a criminally usurious loan. The court found the agreement obligating fixed
daily payments with no reconciliation, a finite term, and recourse upon bankruptcy
established an absolute repayment loan exceeding the criminal usury rate. The denial of
declaratory relief was affirmed for lack of a counterclaim. The order was modified
accordingly and affirmed with costs to defendants.
