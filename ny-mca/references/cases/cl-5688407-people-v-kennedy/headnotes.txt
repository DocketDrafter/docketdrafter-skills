In this criminal prosecution for conspiracy and criminal usury, the defendant challenged
the admission of two pocket diaries offered as business records under CPLR 4518 (a) to
prove his involvement as a loanshark’s silent partner. The Court held that although
records of a criminal enterprise can qualify as business records, the People failed to
establish a sufficient foundation for these diaries’ admission. The expert witness did
not demonstrate that the diaries were kept in the regular course of business or
according to a routine, systematic procedure, nor that entries were contemporaneous. The
Appellate Division’s order reversing the conviction and ordering a new trial was
affirmed.
