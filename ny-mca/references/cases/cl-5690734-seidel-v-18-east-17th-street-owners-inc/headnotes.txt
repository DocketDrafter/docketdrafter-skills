In this mortgage foreclosure action, plaintiff-lenders sought enforcement of a $225,000
bond secured by a second mortgage that was found to bear a usurious 28.6% interest rate.
Defendant cooperative corporation borrowers asserted a usury defense. The court held
that the borrowers were not precluded from raising usury despite conveying the property
subject to the mortgage, as the conveyance was part of the original loan agreement and
the borrowers were effectively the original parties. The plaintiffs’ estoppel in pais
claim failed because the lender suffered no injury from alleged misrepresentations. The
court reversed the Appellate Division, dismissed the complaint, and ordered cancellation
of the bond and mortgage.
