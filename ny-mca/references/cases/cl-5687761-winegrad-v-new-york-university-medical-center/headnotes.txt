In a medical malpractice action, plaintiffs alleged negligence by defendants during
surgery and treatment resulting in injury. The defendants moved for summary judgment,
supported only by affidavits conclusorily denying deviation from accepted medical
practices and causation, while plaintiffs sought to strike defendants’ answers due to
deposition defaults. The Special Term denied summary judgment and granted plaintiffs’
motion, but the Appellate Division reversed and dismissed the complaint. The Court of
Appeals reversed the Appellate Division, holding that defendants failed to make a prima
facie showing entitling them to summary judgment, as their conclusory assertions did not
negate material issues of fact regarding negligence and causation. The case was remitted
for further proceedings.
