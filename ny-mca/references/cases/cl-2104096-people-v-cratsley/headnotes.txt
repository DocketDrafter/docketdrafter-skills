In People v. Cratsley, the court affirmed defendant's conviction for third-degree rape
under Penal Law § 130.25(1), where the issue was whether the 33-year-old complainant
with mental retardation was legally incapable of consenting to sexual intercourse. The
court held that legal incapacity must be proven case-by-case, requiring proof that the
victim was "mentally defective" and incapable of appraising the nature of her conduct.
The jury’s determination, based on testimony from the complainant, family, and a
counselor familiar with her daily functioning, was sufficient without expert testimony.
The court also upheld admission of a psychologist’s IQ report as a business record under
CPLR 4518(a) based on foundation testimony showing it was routinely prepared and relied
upon by the treating agency. The order of the Appellate Division was affirmed.
