In a breach of contract action, the Supreme Court, Kings County, granted the plaintiff
True Business Funding, LLC’s cross-motion for summary judgment and denied the defendant
Guerrero A Construction Corp., LLC’s motion to dismiss, holding the transaction was a
purchase of future receivables not a criminally usurious loan. The court applied the
standard that usury requires a loan or forbearance and determined the agreement’s
indefinite term, reconciliation clause, and bankruptcy provision negated absolute
entitlement to repayment. The appellate court affirmed the judgment awarding damages of
$93,651.88 to the plaintiff and costs, concluding no triable issue of fact existed.
