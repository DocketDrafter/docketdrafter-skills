In an action to recover damages for breach of contract related to the assignment of an
installment contract for a vehicle purchase, the defendant appealed from an order
granting the plaintiff's motion for summary judgment. The court applied the standard
requiring that a plaintiff in a breach-of-contract claim must establish standing by
proving party status or valid assignment with proper evidentiary foundation. The
affidavit offered to establish the plaintiff’s standing was inadequate because the
affiant lacked personal knowledge of the relevant business practices and did not
demonstrate incorporation of the contract or assignment into the plaintiff’s records.
The court reversed the order and denied summary judgment.
