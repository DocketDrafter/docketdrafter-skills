In a breach of contract action, the plaintiff moved to dismiss the defendants’
affirmative defenses and counterclaim alleging the transaction was a criminally usurious
loan, and for summary judgment. The agreement involved the plaintiff purchasing the
defendant’s future receivables with repayment obligations capped but guaranteed
regardless of actual revenue. The Supreme Court denied dismissal of the defensive claims
and summary judgment but mistakenly denied dismissal of the counterclaim itself. The
Appellate Division modified the order to grant dismissal of the counterclaim but
affirmed denial of the motion to dismiss affirmative defenses and for summary judgment,
finding triable issues on usury defenses.
