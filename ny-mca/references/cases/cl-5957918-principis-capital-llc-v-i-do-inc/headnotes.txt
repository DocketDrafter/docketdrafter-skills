In a breach of contract action, the plaintiff appealed from an order denying its motion
for summary judgment on the complaint and dismissing the defendants' affirmative
defenses and counterclaims. The agreement involved the plaintiff purchasing an interest
in the defendant’s future receivables with payments contingent on monthly sales revenue,
lacking a finite term or bankruptcy default provisions. Applying the standard that a
loan must require absolute repayment to be usurious, the court held the transaction was
not a loan and the plaintiff was entitled to judgment as a matter of law. The court
reversed and granted the plaintiff’s motion, including dismissal of affirmative defenses
and counterclaims.
