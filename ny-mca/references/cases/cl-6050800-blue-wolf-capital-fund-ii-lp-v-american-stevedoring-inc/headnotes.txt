In this foreclosure action, plaintiff Blue Wolf Capital Fund II, L.P. sought to enforce
a loan secured by defendant American Stevedoring, Inc.’s collateral. The parties
disputed whether the loan was usurious, implicating New York’s civil and criminal usury
statutes. The court applied the legal standard that a loan charging interest above
statutory limits with intent constitutes criminal usury, voiding the transaction. The
court held that based on the loan agreement’s terms, including a $200,000 deposit
characterized as interest, the effective interest rate exceeded the legal maximum,
rendering the loan criminally usurious. Consequently, the court affirmed the dismissal
of the complaint and found the loan and collateral agreement void and unenforceable.
