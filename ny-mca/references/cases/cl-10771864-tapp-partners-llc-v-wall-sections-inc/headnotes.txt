In this breach of contract action, plaintiff moved for summary judgment against
defendants based on an alleged failure to pay amounts due under a revenue purchase
agreement and a personal guarantee. Supreme Court granted plaintiff’s motion, but the
Appellate Division reversed, holding that although plaintiff met its initial burden by
submitting the agreement, financial records, and affidavits, defendants raised a triable
issue of fact regarding damages through an affidavit. The court further held that the
guarantee claim depended on the breach claim’s success, and thus denied plaintiff’s
motion for summary judgment. The appeal was deemed properly taken from the original
order.
