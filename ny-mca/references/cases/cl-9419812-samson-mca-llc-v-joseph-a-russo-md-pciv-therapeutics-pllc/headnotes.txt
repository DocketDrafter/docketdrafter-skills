In this appeal from a judgment awarding plaintiff money damages, the court considered
whether two revenue purchase agreements were enforceable or void as criminally usurious
loans. Plaintiff advanced funds to the entity defendants in exchange for a share of
future business revenues, with no finite term or absolute repayment obligation, and
reconciliation clauses adjusting payments based on actual revenue. The individual
defendants personally guaranteed performance. The court granted plaintiff’s summary
judgment motion and denied defendants’ cross-motion, holding the agreements were revenue
purchase agreements, not loans, because repayment was not absolute, there was no set
term, and plaintiff lacked significant recourse upon bankruptcy. The judgment was
affirmed and an earlier appeal dismissed as moot.
