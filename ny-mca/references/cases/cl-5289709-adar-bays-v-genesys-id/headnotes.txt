In this certified-question proceeding from the Second Circuit, the court addressed
whether a stock conversion option allowing a lender to convert debt into stock at a
fixed discount constitutes interest under New York’s criminal usury law and whether
loans exceeding the 25% usury cap are void ab initio. The court held that loans
exceeding the statutory interest limit, including value attributable to contingent stock
conversion options reasonably determinable at contracting, violate New York Penal Law §
190.40 and General Obligations Law § 5-511, rendering such contracts void from
inception. The court emphasized that valuation of such options is a factual matter and
rejected treating them as so speculative as to be non-interest.
