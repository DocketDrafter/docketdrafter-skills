In this appeal from a December 2021 Supreme Court order denying defendants' motion to
vacate a default judgment, the First Department reviewed personal jurisdiction under New
York’s long-arm statute CPLR 302(a)(2) in a dispute involving an agreement for KN95
masks between New York plaintiff SOS Capital and Pennsylvania corporate defendant
Recycling Paper Partners of PA LLC and its Florida- and Pennsylvania-domiciled
principals. The court held that CPLR 302(a)(2) requires the tortious act to be committed
while physically present in New York and found plaintiff failed to show the individual
defendants made fraudulent misrepresentations within the state. The court modified to
grant vacatur and dismissed the action against the individual defendants but affirmed as
to the corporate defendant.
