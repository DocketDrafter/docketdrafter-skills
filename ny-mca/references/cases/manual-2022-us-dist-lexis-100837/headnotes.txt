In this removed action under 18 U.S.C. § 1962(c), plaintiff Fleetwood Services, LLC
moved for summary judgment on claims including usury under Texas law and RICO against
defendants Richmond Capital Funding, LLC and Robert Giardina. The court applied New York
law to interpret the Fleetwood Agreement and held it constituted a loan, not a true sale
of future receivables, because it imposed an absolute obligation to repay a fixed
Purchased Amount, granted Richmond extensive remedies upon default, and included
personal guaranties. The court denied summary judgment on the usury claim due to
unresolved choice-of-law issues but granted summary judgment on the RICO claim against
Giardina, finding undisputed evidence of usurious lending as a business and resulting
damages. The breach of contract and related claims were not resolved.
