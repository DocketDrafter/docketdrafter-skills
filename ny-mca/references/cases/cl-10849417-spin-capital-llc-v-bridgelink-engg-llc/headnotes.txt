In an action to recover damages for breach of contract and breach of guaranty, the
plaintiff moved to dismiss the defendants’ counterclaims alleging a criminally usurious
loan and for summary judgment. The Supreme Court granted the plaintiff’s CPLR 3211(a)
motion to dismiss the counterclaims and its summary judgment motion, finding the
merchant agreement a purchase of future receivables, not a usurious loan, because
repayment was not absolute—due to an indefinite term and no bankruptcy default clause.
The court entered judgment for the plaintiff in the sum of $8,234,160.50, and the
defendants’ appeal from the order was dismissed as moot. The judgment was affirmed.
