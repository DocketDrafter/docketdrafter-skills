In Bank of N.Y. Mellon v Gordon, 2019 NY Slip Op 02306, the defendant Dushaun Gordon
appealed from a Supreme Court order that granted summary judgment to the plaintiff in a
mortgage foreclosure action, dismissed Gordon's affirmative defenses and counterclaims,
and appointed a referee to compute the amount due. Applying CPLR 3212 standards, the
court held that the plaintiff made a prima facie showing of standing through possession
of the endorsed note but failed to establish Gordon's default due to insufficient
foundation for the payment records. The appellate court modified the order by denying
summary judgment on the complaint and remitted for further proceedings on discovery
motions.
